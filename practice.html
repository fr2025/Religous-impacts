Christianity Prayer: Regular prayer is a central practice, with many Christians praying daily. Some prayers include the Lordâ€™s Prayer or spontaneous, personal prayers. Worship Services: Christians attend church services, typically on Sundays, for communal worship, singing hymns, and hearing sermons. Sacraments: Baptism and Communion (Eucharist) are key sacraments. Baptism is a rite of initiation, and Communion commemorates the Last Supper. Bible Reading: Christians study the Bible, often in daily devotions or as part of church services. Charity and Acts of Service: Many Christians participate in charity work, helping the needy and supporting social justice causes.

Islam Prayer (Salat): Muslims pray five times a day, facing Mecca. These prayers are a direct form of worship and are said at specific times throughout the day. Fasting (Sawm): During the month of Ramadan, Muslims fast from dawn until sunset, refraining from food, drink, and other physical needs. Almsgiving (Zakat): Muslims are required to give a portion of their income to charity, typically 2.5% of savings annually. Pilgrimage (Hajj): If financially and physically able, Muslims must undertake the Hajj pilgrimage to Mecca at least once in their lifetime. Recitation of the Qur'an: Muslims believe in the Qur'an as the literal word of God, and many memorize and recite verses.

Hinduism Puja: Worship, or puja, is a key practice and can be done at home or in temples. It involves offerings, prayers, and rituals to deities. Meditation and Yoga: Hinduism emphasizes meditation and yoga as ways to achieve spiritual growth, inner peace, and connection with the divine. Festivals: Major festivals include Diwali (Festival of Lights), Holi (Festival of Colors), and Navaratri, each with unique rituals and celebrations. Pilgrimages: Hindus often undertake pilgrimages to holy sites such as Varanasi, the Ganges River, and temples dedicated to specific deities. Ahimsa: Many Hindus practice non-violence (ahimsa), including vegetarianism, as part of their commitment to compassion.

Buddhism Meditation: Meditation is a core practice, with different forms (e.g., mindfulness, loving-kindness meditation, or vipassana) aimed at achieving enlightenment (nirvana). Sangha: The community of monks, nuns, and laypeople is central. Buddhists often gather for teachings, discussions, and to support one another in their spiritual journey. Rituals and Offerings: Buddhists offer incense, flowers, and food to Buddha statues as a sign of respect and devotion. Observing Precepts: Buddhists follow moral precepts, including refraining from killing, stealing, lying, sexual misconduct, and intoxication. Mindfulness and Ethical Living: Practicing mindfulness in everyday life and living according to the Eightfold Path (right understanding, right speech, right action, etc.) is emphasized.
